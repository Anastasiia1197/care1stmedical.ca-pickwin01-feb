Healthy Habits and Strategies for Enjoyable Entertainment

Responsible gambling begins with awareness and proper preparation. Set in advance limits for both time and money you wish to allocate to online entertainment. Always treat games as a form of entertainment, never as a way to earn money or recoup losses. Plan regular breaks during sessions and maintain a balance with other hobbies and responsibilities in your life. These are the basic principles of healthy gaming habits.

Warning Signs of Problematic Gaming

Early recognition of warning signs is crucial for maintaining control. Pay attention to signs such as: difficulty stopping, feeling the need to gamble more to achieve the same emotions, lying to friends or family about time or money spent on gambling, financial problems or borrowing money to gamble, and neglecting work, studies, or social contacts. Do you recognise one or more of these signs in yourself or someone else? It is time to take action.

Debunking Common Myths About Gambling

There are persistent myths about gambling that can fuel risky behaviours. It is important to know them:

  • Online gambling is not an investment: The platform always has a statistical advantage in the long term. Gambling to make money is a dangerous mindset.
  • No "sure" strategy exists: No tactic or system can overcome the randomness (RNG) of fair slot games.
  • "Chasing losses" thinking: Losses cannot be recouped by playing longer or more; each round is independent. This concept is known as the "gambler's fallacy".

Support for Loved Ones and Families

Problematic gambling has a significant impact not only on the player themselves but also on their immediate environment. As a family member, partner, or friend, it is important to recognise the signs and know how to approach the person. Open, non-judgmental communication is the first step. There are also specific support groups and helplines for loved ones in Canada.

Important Support Organisations in Canada

  • ConnexOntario: Free and confidential information about mental health, addictions, and problem gambling services in Ontario. Call 1-866-531-2600 (24/7 helpline).
  • Gamblers Anonymous (GA): Runs support groups across Canada based on the 12-step programme. Visit gamblersanonymous.org/meetings
  • Problem Gambling Helpline (Canada): National network providing confidential advice and support. Call 1-866-767-7569.
  • Centre for Addiction and Mental Health (CAMH): Offers resources and treatment options for gambling problems.
  • Responsible Gambling Council (RGC): Provides a range of resources, advice, and a self-assessment tool. Visit responsiblegambling.org.
  • Gambling, Gaming & Technology Use (BC): Support services for British Columbia residents. Call 1-888-795-6111.
